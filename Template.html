This lesson will help to introduce the concept of japanese, its aim is to be educational and fun, because if people don’t wanna come to the club then there is no club. If you’re instructing this make sure you keep it fun and answer questions if possible.

Before you get to the actual instruction, remember that this is a club, you should have everyone introduce themselves and gimme some sort of ice breaker question or 2. Like favorite food and why did they join the Japanese club. It's a good practice to help the club feel more connected. However if someone doesn’t want to share, encourage them to but if they still don't want to then dont force them.

2

2

Use the format

WATASHI WA ______ DESU Have them fill the blank in with their name.

2

Explain the basics of japanese, how characters always start with a vowel and most end with a consonant (Excluding ち(chi) and　つ(tsu). Talk about the 3 alphabets and kanji and what they are used for.

2

2

2

Hiragana - the basis for all Japanese words. The most important one to learn. When one gets more advanced in japanese they will start to see a lot of kanji, but the particles will always be in hiragana.(If people ask what Kanji and particles are just tell then that you are going to explain them later this meeting)

2

Katakana - used for all foreign words. These will never be “abbreviated” into kanji, so if you learn this alphabet you ill be able to pronounce any foreign word. Also they are often able to be understood just by saying them out loud. For example チーズ chizu - cheese.

2

Romaji - this is hiragana but with english letters, this is very helpful for new learners to learn pronunciation. For example Sushi - すし

2

Kanji - characters that don’t really have a pattern they can be used to mean multiple characters or differentiate words. Such as the word あし　can mean either foot or leg depending on what kanji you use. Learning kanji is something that comes with experience with Japanese, it's not something you really study by itself you just learn it while you are learning other parts of the language.

2

2

2

Particles are something that japanese has that english does not. Particles are singular or double characters that mark certain things about the sentence. Subject, object, verb, possessive, ect.

Though there are quite a few particles they will only need to know 2 for right now

2

は pronounced wa - marks the subject

の pronounced no - marks possession

2

Break down this sentence

2

わたし　は　トマス　です。

WATASHI WA TOMASU DESU

2

This is the sentence for the ice breakers, I filled in the blank with my name

2

WATASHI - meaning I or me

WA - particle that shows the subject of the sentence

TOMASU - Name of person

DESU - shows the end of sentence (also you don’t really pronounce the “u” just end it with a longer “s” sound, sort of like dess)
