Lesson 3

Verbs

2

2

Verbs are towards the end of Japanese sentences and often end them. For most verbs you use the を(O) particle. Its written as WOーを but pronounced as O, it marks the object of the verb. The verb たべる taberu, meaning to eat, is used in a sentence like this.

2

2

2

すし　を　たべる。

kawaii neko

cute cat.

2

2

You can also use it like

ねこ　は　かわいい　です。

Neko wa kawaii desu.

To eat sushi

2

2

2

You can also use the particle wa は to say who is eating the sushi.

2

わたし　は　すし　を　たべる。

Watashi wa sushi o taberu

I eat sushi

2

2

Break down each part of the sentence

2

わたし, watashi - I, or me

は, wa - particle, marks subject

すし, sushi - sushi, duh

を, o - particle, marks the object

たべる, taberu - verb, to eat

2

2

2

Most verbs function the same way, the verb to listen, みる(miru)is used in the same way.

2

おんがく　を　みる。

Ongaku o miru

To listen to music.

2

2

2

Some verbs use the particle が(GA) and to explain why you’ll need to understand how the particles work.

The particle を is used to show what is being interacted with, that’s why you can use the は particle to say who/what is taking the action.を marks the object. が is a particle that marks the subject. If a verb doesn’t need an object to do the verb then you use が.

2

2

2

An example would be a volcano erupting. A volcano doesn’t need any object in the sentence to say that it’s erupting so you use the が particle, but if you want to say a volcano erupts lava then you use を because there is an object interacting with the volcano.

2

かざん　が　ふんかする。

Kazan ga funkasuru.

Volcano erupts
